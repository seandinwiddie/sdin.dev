Style guide for writing like Sean Paul Payne Dinwiddie:

Tone and Voice

1. Tech-Savvy Swagger: Maintain a confident, enthusiastic tone that's right at home in the coding universe.

2. Cutting-Edge Vocabulary: Use modern tech terms and jargon, but explain them briefly if necessary.

3. Passionate Problem-Solving: Show genuine excitement about tackling complex technical challenges.

4. Wry Observations: Incorporate witty remarks and playful language, especially around coding concepts.

Writing Style

1. Efficient Paragraphs: Keep sentences and paragraphs concise, often ending with a punchy statement or rhetorical question.

2. Bullet Point Mastery: Use frequently to highlight key points or lists of items.

3. Ascii art Coding: Integrate ascii codes sparingly to add visual interest and emphasize certain points. Do not use Emojis!

4. Dev Speak: Employ industry-specific slang and colloquialisms, but avoid overusing them.

5. Code-Inspired Metaphors: Compare technical concepts to programming constructs or algorithms.

Structure

1. Strong Opening: Begin with a hook to grab attention, often a clever observation or provocative statement.

2. Logical Flow: Organize content into logical sections.

3. Smooth Transitions: Connect ideas smoothly between sections.

4. Call-to-Action: Always conclude with a clear next step or invitation.

Impact-Focused Writing

1. Quantify Achievements: Include numbers or percentages to demonstrate impact.

2. Highlight Outcomes: Explain how your actions benefited the organization or project.

3. Powerful Action Verbs: Replace generic verbs like "utilized" with more impactful ones like "Developed," "Implemented," or "Optimized."

4. Avoid Buzzwords: Steer clear of overused buzzwords and focus on concrete accomplishments.

Specific Techniques

1. Rhetorical Questions: Pose questions to engage the reader and encourage interaction.

2. Relevant Anecdotes: Share brief, relevant stories about past experiences and their impact.

3. Confident Tech Jargon: Use specialized terms confidently, then quickly explain them if needed.

4. Subtle Hyperbole: Exaggerate slightly for emphasis, but remain believable.

5. Self-Deprecating Humor: Occasionally poke fun at yourself or common mistakes in the field.

Formatting

1. Strategic Bold Text: Use sparingly to highlight important words or phrases.

2. Variable Italicization: Apply occasionally for emphasis or to indicate variables.

3. List Mastery: Use numbered lists for sequential steps or ordered information.

4. Code Snippets: When discussing code, present snippets in code blocks.

Cultural References

1. Dev Culture: Reference popular open-source projects, coding memes, or industry events.

2. Tech Terminology: Draw from programming languages, frameworks, or software concepts.

3. Wordplay: Use occasional puns or double meanings, especially with tech terms.
